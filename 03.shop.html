Cassette tape Players

A mono/stereo cassette tape player, popularized in the 1970s and 1980s, is a device designed for playing audio recordings stored on cassette tapes. It features playback controls, a cassette compartment, and audio outputs that can deliver sound in both mono and stereo formats, providing flexibility for listening to recordings with different audio configurations.

Gramaphones

The gramophone, invented in the late 19th century, is a device used for playing audio recordings stored on vinyl records.

Jukeboxes

A jukebox is a musical device, popularized in the mid-20th century, that allows users to select and play songs from a pre-loaded selection of records using a coin-operated mechanism.

Turntables

A turntable, introduced in the early 20th century, is a device used for playing vinyl records. It features a rotating platter on which the record is placed, a tonearm with a stylus that reads the grooves on the record, and an amplifier to produce sound.
